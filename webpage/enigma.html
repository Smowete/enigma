Enigma


Invented in 1919, the Enigma machine was put to lethal use during World War II.

Originally designed as a cipher for encrypting financial communications, commercial Enigmas used three rotors to reroute electrical circuitry and scramble letters into an unintelligible jumble. Encoded messages were decipphered by the recipient's Enigma.

In 1926, the German military modified the commercial Enigma by adding a front plugboard. This modification and others increased the depth of encrypthing from 17,576 to more than 158 quintillion (158*10^18).
